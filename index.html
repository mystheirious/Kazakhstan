Republic of Kazakhstan

Landlocked Country of Central Asia

The largest country in Central Asia and the ninth largest in the world, measuring about 1,820 miles (2,930 km) east to west and 960 miles north to south. It is divided into 5 regions and 14 provinces. These regions are Central Kazakhstan, North Kazakhstan, South Kazakhstan, East Kazakhstan, and Western Kazakhstan.

The Land & Its People

Geography

  • Location: Central Asia, bordering Russia, China, Kyrgyzstan, Uzbekistan, and Turkmenistan.
  • Area: 2,724,900 km².
  • Landscape: Steppes, deserts (Kyzylkum, Betpak-Dala), mountains (Tien Shan, Altai).
  • Major Rivers & Lakes: Irtysh, Ural, Syr Darya rivers; Caspian Sea, Aral Sea, Lake Balkhash.
  • Climate: Continental climate with hot summers and cold winters.

Culture

It is deeply rooted in its nomadic heritage, emphasizing hospitality, traditional music, and equestrian traditions. Nauryz, the Persian New Year, is the most significant festival, celebrating renewal and unity with music, dance, and special dishes like Nauryz kozhe. The country also values dombra music and epic storytelling, which have been passed down for generations. While modern influences shape urban life, Kazakhs continue to honor their rich traditions through yurt celebrations, folk arts, and respect for elders.

Demography

  • Population: About 19 million people.
  • Ethnic Groups: Kazakh (~70%), Russian (~15%), Uzbeks, Ukrainians, Uighurs, others.
  • Languages: Kazakh (official), Russian (widely spoken).
  • Religion: Islam (~70%), Orthodox Christianity (~25%).
  • Urbanization: Major cities include Nur-Sultan (Astana), Almaty, Shymkent, Karaganda.

Economy

Agriculture

Agriculture remains an important sector in Kazakhstan’s economy, contributing significantly to employment and exports. The country is one of the world’s largest wheat producers, with vast farmlands in the northern regions dedicated to grain cultivation. Livestock farming, including cattle and sheep, is also a traditional industry, supporting both domestic consumption and exports.

Industry

Kazakhstan's industrial sector is driven by oil refining, metallurgy, and machinery production, benefiting from the country’s vast natural resources. The mining industry is particularly strong, with Kazakhstan being the world's leading producer of uranium and a major supplier of copper, zinc, and coal. Heavy industries such as steel and chemical production also play a crucial role in the economy.

Trade

Trade is a key component of Kazakhstan’s economy, with exports largely dominated by oil, gas, and minerals. The country has strong economic ties with major trading partners, including China, Russia, and the European Union. While energy resources account for most of its exports, Kazakhstan also imports machinery, electronics, and consumer goods to meet domestic demand.

Resources

Kazakhstan is rich in natural resources, with vast reserves of oil, natural gas, uranium, and various metals. The country is one of the top global producers of uranium, supplying much of the world's nuclear energy sector. Its large fossil fuel reserves drive economic growth, though there is increasing investment in renewable energy sources such as wind and hydroelectric power.
